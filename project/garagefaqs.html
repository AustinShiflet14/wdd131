Frequently Asked Questions:

Start with a car that is drivable and not a full ground-up restoration. It's best to pick a vehicle that's relatively reliable and doesn't overwhelm you.

Research prices for similar cars, be polite with the seller, inspect thoroughly for rust (especially frame rust), and make sure the title and paperwork are in order.

Make a detailed plan and list the steps before starting. Don't rush into dismantling the car without knowing what you want to do and how.

Its okay to ask questions. Use repair manuals, assembly guides, and online resources to learn. Don't forget to reach out to our team!

First thing: LABEL EVERYTHING. It is often overlooked but so necessary for organization. Put things in bags and label them carefully.

Start with a decent socket and wrench set (metric and standard), rubber mallet, hammers, chisels, pliers, tap and die set, power drill, torque wrench, floor jack, jack stands, and safety glasses. Buy specialty tools only as needed.

Avoid major frame welding, structural work, painting, replacing headliners or convertible tops, and complex wiring. Stick to bolt-on parts like wheels, brakes, shocks, and exhaust components.
